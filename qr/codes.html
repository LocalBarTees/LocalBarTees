🖨️ Professional Printing & Laminating Instructions

📄 Printing for Maximum Quality

Printer Settings:

  • Use 300 DPI minimum for crisp, scannable results
  • Set to "High Quality" or "Best" print mode
  • Select "Photo Paper" setting even for regular paper
  • Ensure black ink cartridge is full - low ink causes scanning issues

Paper Selection:

  • Best: 32lb (120gsm) bright white paper or heavier cardstock
  • Good: Premium inkjet paper (24-28lb)
  • Avoid: Regular copy paper, textured paper, or glossy photo paper
  • Outdoor use: Waterproof synthetic paper or vinyl sticker sheets

🏷️ Optimal Laminating Process

CRITICAL ORDER: Print → Laminate → Cut (for multiple QR codes)

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Print your QR codes on quality paper
  2. Allow 5-10 minutes drying time before laminating
  3. Laminate the entire sheet - this creates sealed edges that prevent moisture infiltration
  4. Cut individual QR codes after laminating, leaving 2-3mm sealed border
  5. Round corners slightly to prevent peeling at edges

Laminating Equipment Options:

  • Thermal Laminator: Best results - use 3mil (75 micron) or 5mil (125 micron) pouches
  • Self-Adhesive Laminating Film: Good for small quantities, no machine required
  • Cold Lamination: Prevents heat damage to inkjet prints

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't use glossy lamination - creates glare that prevents scanning
  • Never cut before laminating - exposes edges to moisture damage
  • Avoid air bubbles - use a squeegee or credit card to smooth
  • Don't laminate immediately - let ink dry to prevent smudging
  • Test scan before mass production - ensure QR codes work after lamination
  • Keep 2-3mm sealed border when cutting - prevents delamination

🔬 Quality Control Checklist

  • ✅ Print test copy and verify QR code scans with multiple devices
  • ✅ Check for adequate contrast - dark QR code on light background
  • ✅ Ensure no blurriness or pixelation at actual size
  • ✅ Verify lamination has no air bubbles or wrinkles
  • ✅ Test durability - laminated codes should resist water droplets
  • ✅ Confirm proper cutting with sealed edges maintained

📱 Testing & Validation

Before Mass Production:

  • Test with iPhone Camera app and Android camera
  • Try scanning from different angles (0°, 30°, 45°)
  • Test under various lighting conditions (indoor, outdoor, fluorescent)
  • Verify scanning works from 6 inches to 3 feet away
  • Check that older smartphones (3+ years) can still scan

After Lamination:

  • Confirm no glare interferes with scanning
  • Test water resistance - spray lightly and scan
  • Verify edges remain sealed after cutting

💡 Professional Tip

For business-critical applications, always produce a small test batch first. Print 5-10 QR codes, laminate using your chosen method, and test them in the actual environment where they'll be used. This prevents costly mistakes and ensures optimal performance.
